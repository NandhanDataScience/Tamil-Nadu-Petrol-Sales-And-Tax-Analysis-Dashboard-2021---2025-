# ⛽ Tamil Nadu Fuel Sales & Tax Analysis (2021–2025)

## 📌 Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing fuel sales, pricing trends, and tax structure in Tamil Nadu from 2021 to 2025. The goal is to uncover patterns in fuel demand, understand price composition, and evaluate the impact of government taxation on retail fuel prices.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze fuel sales trends over time (yearly, monthly, weekly)
* Understand fluctuations in retail fuel prices
* Break down the tax structure (Central vs State contributions)
* Compare market share among major oil companies
* Identify key insights for decision-making

---

## 🗂️ Dataset Description

The dataset includes:

* Fuel sales (Litres & Metric Tons)
* Retail price of fuel
* Tax components:

  * Central Excise Duty
  * State VAT
  * Dealer Commission
  * Other charges
* Government revenue split (Central vs State)
* Company-wise sales data:

  * Indian Oil Corporation (IOC)
  * Bharat Petroleum (BPCL)
  * Hindustan Petroleum (HPCL)

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* Microsoft Power BI (Data Visualization & Dashboarding)
* Microsoft Excel / CSV (Data Source)

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### 🔹 Sales & Demand Analysis

* Year-wise fuel sales trend (2021–2025)
* Monthly demand distribution
* Day-of-week sales patterns

### 🔹 Company-wise Analysis

* Market share comparison among IOC, BPCL, and HPCL
* Monthly sales distribution by company

### 🔹 Price & Tax Structure

* Breakdown of fuel price components
* Trend of tax percentage over time
* Comparison of Central and State government revenue

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* Fuel demand shows a steady increase over the years
* Higher sales observed during weekends compared to weekdays
* Taxes constitute a significant portion of retail fuel price
* Indian Oil Corporation (IOC) holds the largest market share (~45%)
* State government revenue contribution is consistently higher than central share

---

## 📈 Conclusion

The analysis highlights that fuel consumption in Tamil Nadu is steadily growing, while fuel prices are significantly influenced by taxation policies. Understanding these trends can help policymakers and businesses make informed decisions.
